About this website


Use this website to collaborate with other St. Paul community members to create more green spaces in your area. You can check out the interactive map and join folks already collaborating on new greenspace projects nearby, or you can indicate a location on the map that would make for great green space and wait for other community members to express interest in this project and collaborate on its creation.

Creating new green spaces often requires funding and approval from the St. Paul Parks and Recreation Organization and/or the St. Paul City Council. Resources for accessing these entities can be found below.

There are several local nonprofits devoted to creating and preserving local green spaces in St. Paul and the surrounding metro area. These nonprofits can help fund your project and also help advocate for your project to St. Paul Parks and Rec or the City Council. Information about these organizations and how to contact them is also provided below.

Resources for next steps


Taking the first steps to create a new green space can be overwhelming. A good place to start is reaching out to local nonprofits, Parks and Recreation Organizations, and city or community councils. Utilize these resources and connections to get started.


  • NON-PROFIT PARTNERS
  • CITY PARKS & RECREATION ORGANIZATIONS
  • COUNCILS

The Trust For Public Land

The Trust for Public Land creates parks and protects land for people, ensuring healthy, livable communities for generations to come.

LEARN MORE

St. Paul Parks and Recreation Conservancy

The Saint Paul Parks Conservancy raises funds and partners with the Department of Parks and Recreation and the community to enhance and expand parks and recreational facilities that meet the changing needs of people in our diverse and growing city.

LEARN MORE

Minnesota Land Trust

The Minnesota Land Trust works to protect and restore the natural places we all cherish and engage the next generation in the outdoors.

LEARN MORE

Saint Paul Parks and Recreation Department

St. Paul Parks and Recreation accepts Community Project Proposals , detailed plans of the logistics for a new park.

LEARN MORE

Urban Food Production

Cultivating community gardens builds connections within a community, provides business and employment opportunities, educates community members, and generates a local food source for the community. For more information and a guide to get started, check out St. Paul Parks and Recreation site.

LEARN MORE

Union Park District Council

The Union Park District Council is a nonprofit neighborhood organization that elevates community voices in local decision-making. Through our committees, events, and community outreach, we engage all those who live, work, learn and play in Union Park.

LEARN MORE

Lexington Hamline Community Council

The purpose of the Lexington-Hamline Community Council is to improve the quality of life and bring about positive community change in the Lexington-Hamline neighborhood of St. Paul.
